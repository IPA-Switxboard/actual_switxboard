Ennezlat

Development of an integrated sustainable Farm, Ecolodge and Produce Processing Facility

Ennezlat is an integrated agricultural project under development in Tataouine, in South-East Tunisia. Ennezlat adopts a holistic approach to development.Using :

  • The principles of social entrepreneurship, it relies on innovative technology for process optimization
  • The sustainable use of natural resources and the production
  • The well-being and up-skilling of the local community, especially women and young people.

It aims at building strong communities through reinforcing their economic, social and environmental resilience.

Ennezlat is an integrated agricultural project under development in Tataouine, in South-East Tunisia. It aims at building strong communities through reinforcing their economic, social and environmental resilience.

The project under development in Tatatouine is currently unused land of some 500 Hectares which can be expanded to over 1000 hectares or more. Some 200 hectares are in one lot. Water is available by deep wells with sufficient reserves and there are nearby irrigated farms. Although the land is administratively dependent on the village of Douiret it is suited near the main road and accessible from other areas and the nearby city of Tataouine. The project aims at developing and introducing sustainable agriculture, processing of agricultural and farm products and new technologies through an integrated concept. It is expected that a number of villages, entrepreneurs and farmers will adopt the techniques and technologies based on the experiences and successes of the farm. The plan is to establish an Ecolodge and -village combined with a number of workshops, processing plants and small industries to create added value to the products of the farm but also to create jobs and provide facilities for small producers in the region. The required energy for the farm, Ecovillage and production sites will be generated by solar energy and biogas The project is based on the successful model implemented by SEKEM in Egypt and other similar projects.


Agriculture :

The project will be developed in close cooperation with the “Institut des Regions Arides” in Tataouine which has established a pilot farm integrating tree, fruit and vegetables with animal husbandry testing adaptability of tree varieties and breeds. Moringa has a great potential for development in the region and provides as cash crop a number of opportunities, including medical, cosmetic, foods and animal fodder. Dates, figs and olives are traditionally grown. Other crops and fruits such as mango or papaya have high potential. Medical plants, herbs and spices have high potential. Vegetables can be grown. Vertical farming and aquaponics can be introduced. The land would provide for goat, sheep and camel rearing.

Production:

The farm products will be processed on site and a number of products be marketed for the national and international market under a common branding and following the necessary ISO standards. These will be ranging from Moringa products, essential oils, herbs and spices, olive and date products and other processed fruits and vegetables. Milk and meat will be processed. The produce by local farmers can be processed on site and be included in the sales and marketing structure.

Energy:

With the intention to be energy independent and sustainable the solar power production will be central provider of energy. Power generation by biogas is planned with a plant transforming agricultural waste. Energy will be used to power the Ecolodge and -village, water pumps and irrigation, cooling facilities, water treatment as well as machinery and equipment needed to process the agricultural produce.

Eco-Lodge and Village:

Sustainable tourism has great potential in the region offering a wealth of activities in exploring the desert, discovering the rich cultural and archeological heritage, palaeontology, astronomy, film / photo (Starwars) and arts. The lodge and the village will offer accommodation for tourism (short and long stays), skill based volunteers, worker families and offer space for training and workshops.

Training and Eduation:

The lodge and village will serve as training and information centre with the objective to offer at least 30 courses and trainings per year for local entrepreneurs and producers, students and other external participants. In combination with the workshops planned in the historic village of Douiret additional activities and workshops and courses are possible.

Employment:

In the initial stage of the farm establishment and construction some 50 local workers will employed on the farm in addition to the management structure. Additional 50 persons will be employed in year 2 for the production and transformation of the farm produce. Some 50 jobs will be created with the establishment of the lodge and the village. 50% of the employees will be women. Additional income for local producers and suppliers will be ensured. In total some 150 persons will be directly employed.

Partners and Structures:

The main agricultural project will be carried by a commercial company with a shareholding participation of landowners and workers. The eco-lodge and the processing plants for agricultural produce will be established as a cooperative or social enterprise in cooperation with partners, local producers and workers.
