Tunisian Return Reintegration Project

  • Milestone 1 : Building a project and outreach structure in Germany (GESA)
  • Milestone 2: Identifying the support requirements & opportunities for return candidates, engaging in dialogue & preparing return (GESA)
  • Milestone 3: Building a project implementation and outreach structure in Tunisia (IPA)
  • Milestone 4: Reception, Integration, Mentorship for up to 200 forced returnees per year in Tunisia
  • Milestone 5: Establishment of social service provider entity in Tunisia

Description

Amongst the approximately 80.000 Tunisians living in Germany a considerable number of persons have no regular status and are obliged to leave the German territory. While an opportunity is given by the authorities to return voluntarily with an assistance package prior to return enforcement, nearly up to 500 individuals per year are forcibly returned from Germany to Tunisia based on the bi-lateral re-admission agreennent between the two govern- ments. The structures established in Tunisia (Tunis/Sfax) under the AVRR programmes through the GIZ/OFI/ANETI cooperation, are designed for returnees willing to cooperate and prepared to reintegrate, but cannot provide the very specific psychological and social support needed for forced returnees. These returnees are difficult to access as they may avoid any contact with authorities, reject any counseling and advice and often attempt to return to Europe as soon as possible through irregular means. Social workers and counselors in Germany and in Tunisia report considerable violence and behavioral issues beyond the capacity of the existing counseling and reception structures. In general the issue of forced returns is highly emotional and the environment of enforcement structures and pro- cedures does not facilitate a constructive dialogue. At present humanitarian organizations are reluctant to engage in the context of forced returns ei- ther prior to departure or post return, authorities are suspicious and families and communities rejecting the person as such a return is considered a failure. The problem this project attempts specifically to address is to prevent that these individuals are further drawn into the cycle and logic of societal ex- clusion, irregular migration and potentially crime, but rather be given a chance to reintegrate into society, their families and join positive social net- works and income generating activities or jobs.
Therefore the project aims on one hand to get in contact with Tunisians in detention and accompany them. Furthermore, the project will pilot a "street- worker" approach and attempt to reach these persons as early as possible prior to departure and possibly as late as in detention. On the other hand engage on the receiving side through a team of social workers, specifically trained and capable of dealing with extremely vulnerable cases, violence, trauma and social issues. Based on the engagement with the returnee in all stages of return and a continued mentoring process the aim is a plug- and-play referral mechanism with the job and skill development programs available through the GIZ centers in Tunis and Sfax or other providers in Tunisia. In the course of implementation a sustainable social enterprise will be established, modeled on the example of social service provider entities in Eu- rope. This entity will be a first to help address the gap in Tunisia between state agencies and civil society as regards social service provision and so- cioeconomic integration of vulnerable individuals and families in the society.

--Objective--

Support and accompany the sustainable reintegration of up to 200 Tunisians per year (50 in Germany, 150 in Tunisia) forced to return from Germany with enhanced personalized counseling, outreach and socio/economic measures and establishing a sustainable social service provider structure (so- cial enterprise model) in Tunisia.

Milestone 1 : Building a project and outreach structure in Germany (GESA)

GESA gGmbH in Wuppertal, specialized in the provision of reintegration assistance and job placement for persons in difficulty, will ensure the overall management and oversight of the project. GESA is experienced in coordination, financing, administration and billing of projects which are funded by the EU (i.e. ESF), Germany (i.e. BAMF, BMAS), federal states (i.e. MKFFI, MAGS) or other finance providers. The existing GESA administrative/fi- nancial and personnel structure under the overall guidance of the CEO and relevant heads of departments is at the disposal of the project. GESA will employ a case (streetworker) worker with the specific task to engage with the persons of concern (POC) prior to return, including in deten- tion pending deportation. The initial pilot focus region will be North Rhine-Westphalia to test the approach (prior to potential expansion to other regions). GESA together with its local partner in Tunisia, IPA-Switxboard SUARL will identify a joint project coordinator for the overall management of project components in Germany and Tunisia being based out of Tunisia but with extended engagement in Germany especially in the setup phase to build structure and relationships..

Milestone 2: Identifying the support requirements & opportunities for return candidates, engaging in dialogue & preparing return (GESA)

Considering strong resistance by POC to positively engage, the GESA caseworker will establish contact and access the target group through Tunisian community, peers, friends and families. The fact that the POC are faced with multiple challenges (“loser image”, incurred debt, criminal record, etc.) will need to be considered in all stages of the project regarding a sustainable successful return. The aim is to develop an individual return profile and plan based on a structured discussion identifying needs, challenges and potential for reintegra- tion. This social work follows a “buddy peer approach” as it involves an in-depth understanding of the social context of the individuals and the Tunisian diaspora in Germany. Ideally this dialogue leads to the creation of an individual checklist for the arrival, which includes destination location and context, social contacts, job preferences, apartment search and need for assistance with bureaucracy in order to facilitate reintegration. Addi- tionally POC will receive necessary information as well as options regarding further training programs on the IPA points of contact and process upon arrival. Another effect is to possibly deescalate the forced return process of removal and to begin building a positive relationship by focusing e.g. on preparatory activities.

Milestone 3: Building a project implementation and outreach structure in Tunisia (IPA)

IPA-Switxboard Tunisia SUARL supported by IPA GmbH in Vienna will be subcontracted by GESA to ensure implementation and operationalisation of the project throughout year 1 pending the establishment of the planned local social enterprise. IPA ensures management, administration and coor- dination of staff and stakeholders on site in Tunisia, including recruitment of the overall GESA/IPA Project Coordinator. Within 6-8 weeks IPA will re- cruit project staff, including local coordinator, counselors and social workers and establish project offices in Sfax and Tunis. IPA, will map and evaluate the Tunisian ecosystem of service providers, experts and municipalities, including the identification of focal points in all Governorates for referral and mentorship tailored to the special needs of the POC. It is intended to establish operational synergies with ongoing efforts by the European Union supporting the establishment and building of capacity of an inter-ministerial mechanism supporting voluntary return and reintegration hosted by the Office of the Overseas Tunisians (OTE) in cooperation with the Ministry of Social Affairs, the Agency for Employment (ANETI) and the “German-Tunisian Centre for Jobs, Migration and Integration” (Centre Tuniso-Allemand). A strong coordination and complementarity with other local and international service providers currently assisting with training, counselling and social rehabilitation in Tunisia will be sought to build a performant local referral and mentorship ecosystem throughout the country. In a series of meetings and small workshops these focal points and officials will be sensitised to the issue and briefed on the project and importance of cooperation and referrals to enhance prospects for integration. Separate information and sensitisation sessions will be conducted with members of the law enforcement agencies and Tunisian Ministry of Interior if feasible.
