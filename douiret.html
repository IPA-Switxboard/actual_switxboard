The Douiret Workshop Series

Collective Project Design Through Residential Workshops

From Tales of Caves, Khsars and Caravans to the Rebirth of the Desert City

Douiret, a Berber mountain town over 600 years old, is primarily deserted but represents the his- tory of trade and the region as a critical former caravan stop between Libya and the north of the Maghreb. The partly run-down dwellings of the original inhabitants stretch over three kilometres as "troglodytes" or cave houses carved into the mountain. On the central mountain, built using clay, were the granaries or "Khsar". Inhabitants cultivated olives and corn on the plains. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douiret The region and troglodytes are being considered to join the UNESCO World Heritage List..

Workshop Activities and Themes

  • Architecture & Design: Restoration and maintenance of troglodytes, khsars, mosques, shops, olive oil mills and other buildings
  • Tourism: Development of sustainable tourism facilities, hiking trails, development of con- cepts etc
  • Agriculture: Maintenance and construction of agricultural dams for water and irrigation management
  • Greening of the desert, planting of trees, cultivation permaculture
  • Energy production and distribution: Renewable energies
